## Notes

Created and maintained with daily feedings of 1 cup flour + ½ cup water after discard.
**Libation while feeding**: A small glass of whatever you please—it is, after all, a living courtier deserving of tribute.
### How to Create Thy Royal Starter (Cups & Spoons Edition)
Begin on Day 1, and proceed daily. Use a clean jar or crock (1-quart size is perfect).
- **Day 1**: Mix 1 cup flour (all-purpose or bread flour is fine; whole wheat or rye speeds things up) + ½ cup lukewarm water (not hot—yeast dies above 120°F). Stir until smooth like thick pancake batter. Cover loosely with a lid or cloth. Place in a warm spot (70–80°F ideal).
- **Day 2**: It may look unchanged or have a few bubbles. Discard nothing yet. Feed: Add 1 cup flour + ½ cup lukewarm water. Stir well.
- **Day 3–5**: Now the fun begins! You’ll see bubbles, smell a tangy or boozy scent. Each day:
  Discard all but ½ cup of the mixture (give it to the royal compost or fry into discard pancakes—see below).
  Feed the remaining ½ cup with 1 cup flour + ½ cup lukewarm water. Stir, cover loosely.
- **Day 6–10**: By now it should double in size within 4–8 hours after feeding, smell pleasantly sour (like yogurt or beer), and have lots of bubbles. It is ready when it reliably doubles and passes the “float test”: drop a teaspoon into water—if it floats, it’s strong enough to bake!
### Daily Care of Thy Mature Starter
- **Counter Life** (for frequent baking): Feed daily—discard down to ½ cup, add 1 cup flour + ½ cup water.
- **Fridge Life** (for occasional baking): Feed once a week. Let sit at room temp 1–2 hours after feeding, then refrigerate. When ready to bake, take out, feed twice at room temp before using.
### Royal Discard Treats (Waste Not!)
That discarded starter is gold:
- **Sourdough Pancakes**: 1 cup discard + 1 egg + 2 Tbsp sugar + 2 Tbsp melted butter + ½ tsp baking soda + pinch salt. Cook like regular pancakes—heavenly tang.
- **Crackers**: Mix discard with olive oil, herbs, salt—roll thin, bake until crisp.
- **Muffins, biscuits, brownies**—it enhances nearly everything.
### Troubleshooting Thy Starter
- Smells like nail polish? Too much acid—feed more often or move to cooler spot.
- Hooches (gray liquid on top)? It’s hungry—pour off or stir in, then feed.
- No bubbles after 10 days? Try rye flour.
