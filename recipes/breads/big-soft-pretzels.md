## Ingredients

- 4½–5 cups bread flour
- 1½ cups warm water (110°F / 43°C)
- 2 Tbsp brown sugar or barley malt syrup (malt gives authentic flavor)
- 2¼ tsp instant yeast
- For denser pretzels try 1½ tsp. It'll still rise reliably (just slower, maybe 90–120 minutes instead of 60–90), but you'll get noticeably less puff. If they're still too fluffy, go down to 1 tsp next time for even denser results (rise could take 2+ hours, which also builds better flavor through longer fermentation).
- 2 tsp salt
- 4 Tbsp unsalted butter, melted
- Baking soda bath (⅔ cup soda in 10 cups water)
- Coarse salt topping

### Alkaline Bath (the secret to that dark, shiny crust)
- 10 cups water
- ⅔ cup baking soda
- (Food-grade lye is traditional and superior, but baking soda is safe and effective at home)

### Topping
- Coarse pretzel salt (or kosher salt)
- Melted butter for brushing after baking

## Method

1. Knead, rise 60–90 min, shape, soda bath 20–30 seconds each. Bake **12–15 minutes at 450°F** until deep mahogany. Brush with butter.
2. Mix warm water, sugar/malt, and yeast. Let sit 5 minutes until foamy.
3. Add melted butter, salt, and 4 cups flour. Mix until a shaggy dough forms.
4. Knead 8–10 minutes (by hand or mixer), adding flour as needed until smooth and slightly tacky but not sticky.
5. Place in oiled bowl, cover, rise 60–90 minutes until doubled.
6. Preheat oven to 450°F (230°C). Line two sheets with parchment.
7. Bring the 10 cups water + baking soda to a rolling boil.
8. Divide dough into 8 (large) or 12 (medium) pieces. Roll each into a 24–30 inch rope (longer for bigger pretzels).
9. Shape into classic pretzel knot: U-shape → cross ends twice → fold down onto the curve.
10. Carefully dip each pretzel in the boiling baking soda bath for 20–30 seconds, flipping once. Remove with slotted spatula, let excess drip off.
11. Place on parchment, sprinkle generously with coarse salt.
12. Bake 12–15 minutes until deep mahogany brown (rotate sheets halfway).
13. Brush immediately with melted butter. Devour while hot.
