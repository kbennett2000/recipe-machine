## Ingredients

- 1 1/2 cups (180 g.) all-purpose flour
- 1 Tbsp. granulated sugar
- 1/4 tsp. kosher salt
- 1/2 cup (1 stick) cold unsalted butter, cut into 1/2" cubes
- 1/4 cup ice cold water, plus more as needed

## Method

1. ##### Making the Dough:
2. In a large bowl, whisk flour, sugar, and salt.
3. Add butter and toss to coat in flour.
4. Using your hands, work butter into flour mixture until walnut-sized pieces form.
5. Add ice water and stir and squeeze with your hands to form a dough.
6. Add more water, 1 tablespoon at a time as needed, until dough holds together; it shouldn’t feel sticky but will easily hold together when squeezed.
7. ##### Rolling Out and Shaping the Dough:
8. Let the chilled dough disc sit at room temperature for 5-10 minutes to make it easier to roll without cracking.
9. On a lightly floured surface, roll the dough into a 12" circle about 1/8" thick.
10. If it sticks, add a bit more flour; if it feels too soft, chill it for 10-15 minutes.
11. Carefully transfer to a 9" pie plate (metal is best for even baking).
12. Gently press it into the bottom and sides without stretching it.
13. Trim the overhang to about 1", fold the excess under itself, and crimp the edges with your fingers or a fork for a decorative look.
14. Prick the bottom and sides all over with a fork (this prevents bubbling) (unless it's a double-crust pie).
15. Chill the shaped crust in the fridge for at least 30 minutes (or freezer for 10 minutes) to help it hold its shape and stay flaky during baking.
16. Tip: Always keep things cold—chill the dough anytime it starts feeling warm or sticky to maintain those butter pockets for flakiness. Bake the pie on a preheated baking sheet to help the bottom set quickly and avoid sogginess.
17. ##### Baking Instructions by Pie Type:
18. Fully Blind-Baked Crust (for pies with no-bake or short-bake fillings, like lemon meringue, chocolate pudding, coconut cream, or banana cream pie)
19. This fully pre-bakes the crust so it stays crisp under wet fillings.
20. Preheat oven to 375°F with a baking sheet on the center rack.
21. Line the chilled crust with parchment paper or foil, and fill it completely with pie weights, dry beans, or rice (right up to the edges to prevent slumping).
22. Bake for 25-30 minutes, until the edges are set and just starting to turn golden.
23. Carefully remove the parchment and weights. If the bottom has puffed, gently prick it again.
24. Return to the oven and bake for 10-15 minutes more, until the bottom is dry, golden, and fully cooked through.
25. Let cool completely on a wire rack before adding filling (this prevents sogginess).
26. Par-Baked Crust (for pies where the filling bakes in the oven but you want to prevent a soggy bottom, like quiche, pumpkin pie, or pecan pie)
27. This partially pre-bakes the crust—it's similar to blind baking but shorter, since the filling will finish cooking it.
28. Preheat oven to 375°F with a baking sheet on the center rack.
29. Line the chilled crust with parchment or foil, and fill with weights.
30. Bake for 15-20 minutes, until the edges are set and lightly golden.
31. Remove the parchment and weights.
32. Bake for 5-10 minutes more, until the bottom looks dry and set but not fully browned.
33. Add your filling immediately while the crust is hot (this helps seal it), then bake according to your filling's recipe.
34. Double-Crust Pie (for fruit pies like apple, cherry, or berry—double the dough recipe and divide into two discs before chilling)
35. No pre-baking needed; the crust bakes with the filling.
36. Prepare the bottom crust as above (roll, fit, chill—no pricking).
37. Add your filling (e.g., sliced fruit mixed with sugar, spices, and a thickener like cornstarch).
38. Roll out the second disc into a 12" circle. Place it over the filling, or cut into strips for a lattice top.
39. Trim edges to 1" overhang, fold under, crimp to seal, and cut a few slits in the top for steam to escape.
40. Brush the top with an egg wash (1 egg beaten with 1 tbsp water) for shine, and sprinkle with coarse sugar if desired.
41. Preheat oven to 425°F with a baking sheet on the center rack.
42. Place the pie on the hot sheet and bake for 20 minutes.
43. Reduce temperature to 375°F and bake for 40-50 minutes more, until the filling is bubbly and the crust is deeply golden.
44. If the edges brown too quickly, cover them loosely with foil halfway through.
45. Let cool on a wire rack for at least 2 hours (ideally 4+) before slicing to let the filling set.
